Ramen

Description

The recipe includes of this particular dish is an easy homemade ramen recipe inspired by traditonal Japanese ramen. It uses a flavorful broth that is complimented by seasoned chicken and veggies, served in a bowl with noodles and to touch it all off with a boiled egg.

This recipe should not take you no more than an hour, making this recipe simple and quick to enjoy. Below, is the list of ingredients used to make this recipe

Ingredients

  • 2 chicken breast(boneless)
  • 1 Tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tsp seasme or vegetable oil
  • 2 tsp fresh ginger, minced
  • 1 Tbsp fresh garlic, minced
  • 3 Tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 Tbsp mirin
  • 4 cups rich chicken stock
  • 1 oz dried shitake mushrooms (or 1/2 cup fresh)
  • 1-2 tsp sea salt, to taste
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup scallions, sliced
  • 2 (3 oz) packs dried ramen noodles

Steps

COOK THE CHICKEN
  1. Preheat the oven to 375F. Season the chicken generously with salt and pepper.
  2. Melt the butter in a large oven-safe skillet over medium heat. Add the chicken, skinside down, and cook until skin is golden brown and releases easily from the pan, about 5-7 minutes. Flip the chicken over and cook for another 4-5 minutes, until golden.
  3. Transfer the skiller to the oven and roast for 15-20 minutes, until the chocken is cooked through. Remove from the oven, transfer the chicken to the plate, and cover the foil until ready to serve.
MAKE THE RAMEN BROTH
  1. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat until shimmering. Add the garlic and ginger, and cook for a few minutes until softened. Add the soy and sauce and mirin, and stir to combine. Cook for another minute.
  2. Add the stock, cover, and bring to a boil. Remove the lid, and let simmer uncovered for 5 minutes, then addd the dried mushrooms. Simmmer gently for another 10 minutes, and season with salt, to taste.
MAKE THE SOFT-BOILED EGG
  1. Fill a pot with enough water to cover the eggs, and bring to a boil. gently lower the eggs(still cold from the fridge) into the boiling water, and let simmer for 7 minutes(for a slightly-runny yolk) or 8 minutes(for a soft, but set-up yolk).
  2. Meanwhile, fill a large bowil with ice water. When the timer finishes, transfer the eggs to the ice bath to stop the cooking process. Wait at least 5 minutes, or until xool enough to handle, then carefully peel away the shell and slice in half, lengthwise. Set aside until ready to serve.
ASSEMBLE THE RAMEN BOWL
  1. Meanwhile, chop the scallions and jalepenos(if using). Slice the chicken into thin pieces. Set aside. When the eggs finish cooking, add the ramen noodles to the boiling water.
  2. Cook for 2-3 minutes, until soft, then divide the noodles into a two large bowls. Add the sliced chicken and the ramen broth. top with the fresh scallions, jalapeno, and the soft boiled egg. Serve immediately. Enjoy!
